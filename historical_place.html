Temples
Tirumala Venkateswara Temple

Venkateswara Temple is one of the most famous and richest temples in Andhra Pradesh. This temple is located at Tirumala in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh. This divine site is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, worshipped by millions of devotees. From here, Lord Balaji blesses devotees with prosperity, peace, and devotion.

Simhachalam Narasimha Temple

Simhachalam Temple is one of the most sacred Narasimha temples in Andhra Pradesh. This temple is located at Simhachalam in the Visakhapatnam district of Andhra Pradesh. This holy shrine is famous for its fusion of South Indian temple architecture and rich history. From here, Lord Narasimha grants protection, faith, and courage to his devotees.

Kanaka Durga Temple

Kanaka Durga Temple is one of the most revered temples in Andhra Pradesh. This temple is located in Vijayawada, nestled on the Indrakeeladri Hill along the Krishna River. It is dedicated to Goddess Durga, worshipped as Kanaka Durga, the embodiment of power. Devotees believe she fulfills wishes and protects from evil forces.

Srisailam Mallikarjuna Temple

Srisailam Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located in the Kurnool district on the banks of the Krishna River amidst the Nallamala Hills. The temple is revered by both Shaivites and Shaktas for its divine presence. It is a major pilgrimage site known for its spiritual significance and scenic surroundings.

Draksharamam Bhimeswara Swamy Temple

Draksharamam Temple is one of the Pancharama Kshetras dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located in the Konaseema region of East Godavari district. The temple is known for its intricate sculptures and historical significance in South Indian heritage. Pilgrims believe visiting this temple brings peace and spiritual growth.

Gudimallam Parasurameswara Temple

Gudimallam Temple houses one of the oldest Shiva Lingas in the world, dating back to 3rd century BCE. It is located near Tirupati in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh. The Lingam here is unique, bearing a figure of Lord Shiva carved into the front. The temple reflects ancient architectural and spiritual traditions of India.

Ahobilam Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

Ahobilam Temple is a sacred shrine dedicated to Lord Narasimha, the fierce form of Vishnu. Located in the Nallamala Hills in Nandyal district, it has nine forms of Narasimha across two parts—Upper and Lower Ahobilam. It is a center of deep faith and adventure as devotees trek through forests to reach the temples. It holds deep spiritual and mythological significance.

Srikalahasteeswara Shiva Temple

Srikalahasti Temple is a prominent Shiva temple located near Tirupati in Andhra Pradesh. It is known as the “Kashi of the South” and is famous for its Vayu Lingam, representing the wind element. The temple is a major site for Rahu-Ketu dosha remedies and spiritual healing. Its Dravidian architecture and spiritual aura attract thousands of devotees.

Mantralayam Raghavendra Swamy Matha

Mantralayam is a revered pilgrimage site located in the Kurnool district of Andhra Pradesh. It is the resting place of the 17th-century saint Sri Raghavendra Swamy. Devotees come here to seek blessings, miracles, and peace. The temple is situated on the banks of the Tungabhadra River and radiates divine energy.

Annavaram Veera Venkata Satyanarayana Temple

Annavaram Temple is a major pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Veera Venkata Satyanarayana, a form of Vishnu. It is located on the Ratnagiri Hill near the banks of the Pampa River in Andhra Pradesh. Devotees perform Satyanarayana Vratham here to seek divine blessings. The serene hilltop view and rituals make it a spiritually fulfilling destination.
