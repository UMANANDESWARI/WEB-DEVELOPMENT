🧵 Why Mulberry Crop Monitoring Matters

Mulberry leaf quality directly impacts silkworm health, silk yield, and cocoon quality. Monitoring factors like leaf moisture, nutrient content, and pest presence helps sericulture farmers ensure consistent, high-quality leaf production. Early detection leads to better silkworm rearing, reduced crop loss, and increased profitability in sustainable silk farming.

🌿 Recommended Practices for Mulberry Cultivation

Based on your soil's pH, moisture & nutrient levels

🌱 Soil Preparation

Mulberry grows best in well-drained loamy soils with good fertility and pH between 6.2-6.8.

💧 Moisture Management

Maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during leaf production; avoid water stagnation.

🧪 Fertilizer Use

Apply nitrogen-rich fertilizers for better leaf yield; use FYM and compost to enhance organic content.

🌤️ Sunlight & Spacing

Ensure 6-7 hours of sunlight daily and maintain proper spacing (3ft x 3ft) for healthy growth.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions for Mulberry

Based on NPK analysis for leaf yield optimization:

  • Nitrogen (Low): Apply Urea or Ammonium Sulphate to boost leaf growth
  • Phosphorus (Optimal): Maintain current levels, no action needed
  • Potassium (High): Avoid MOP; monitor for leaf burn or curling

🌿 Soil Health Tips for Mulberry Cultivation

  • Apply farmyard manure (FYM) and vermicompost to boost organic content.
  • Rotate mulberry plots with legumes to restore soil nitrogen.
  • Ensure good drainage to avoid root rot in humid conditions.
  • Use mulching to conserve moisture and suppress weeds.
